About the Army

The Army is the largest branch of the U.S. Armed Forces, responsible for land-based military operations. Its mission is to defend the country and its interests by providing prompt, sustained land dominance across the full range of military operations and spectrum of conflict in support of combatant commanders.

History

The Army was established on June 14, 1775, making it the oldest branch of the U.S. military. Throughout its history, the Army has been involved in numerous conflicts and has played a crucial role in shaping the nation.
